About me

I'm a PhD candidate in Computer Science at Stony Brook University, advised by Dr. Owen Rambow.

I am broadly interested in assessing, understanding, and improving the ability for AI to model cognitive state and theory of mind. My recent research focuses specifically on VLMs and how they perform in classical psychology experiments such as human referential tasks. Some of my past research involves developing a technique of improving performance of cosine similarity-based systems using interpretable features by training a neural network to predict a similarity residual, i.e. the error in the similarity predicted by an interpretable system.

In my free time, I'm an amateur powerlifter (2/3/4 plates bench/squat/deadlift, aiming for the 1000 lb club), nature photographer, home cook, and musician—currently learning guitar/bass after playing violin for years. I love exploring New York's hidden trails and parks.
